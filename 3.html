ALL ABOUT VEG KOLHAPURI

Veg Kolhapuri is a popular Indian dish that originated in the city of Kolhapur in Maharashtra, India. It is a spicy and flavorful dish that is loved by vegetarians and non-vegetarians alike. This dish is known for its rich and aromatic gravy made with a blend of spices, vegetables, and coconut. Veg Kolhapuri is a staple in Maharashtrian cuisine and has gained popularity all over India and even internationally.

Recipe

To make Veg Kolhapuri, you would require ingredients:

  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (potato, cauliflower, carrot, peas)
  • 1 onion and 1 tomato, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • 1/2 cup grated coconut and 1/4 cup cashew nuts
  • 2 tablespoons oil and Fresh coriander leaves

Procedure:

  1. In a pan, heat oil and add cumin seeds and mustard seeds. Once they start to splutter, add the finely chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
  2. Add the ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a minute. Then, add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn soft. Add the turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and garam masala powder. Mix well and cook for a minute.
  3. In a separate pan, dry roast the grated coconut and cashew nuts until they turn golden brown. Let it cool and then grind it into a fine paste. Add the mixed vegetables to the pan and mix well with the spices. Cover the pan and let the vegetables cook for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Once the vegetables are cooked, add the coconut-cashew paste to the pan and mix well. Let it cook for another 5 minutes. Add salt to taste and garnish with fresh coriander leaves. Your Veg Kolhapuri is now ready to be served.

Place of Origin

eg Kolhapuri gets its name from the city of Kolhapur, located in the western Indian state of Maharashtra. This city is known for its rich and spicy cuisine and Veg Kolhapuri is a perfect example of that. The dish was originally made with mutton or chicken, but the vegetarian version gained popularity due to its delicious taste and use of local vegetables. The dish has evolved over time and is now a staple in Maharashtrian households and restaurants. It has also gained popularity in other parts of India and is often found on the menus of Indian restaurants worldwide.
