ALL ABOUT BENDAKAYA PULUSU

Bendakaya Pulusu, also known as Okra Stew, is a traditional Indian dish that is popular in the southern states of India. This dish is a perfect blend of tangy and spicy flavors, making it a favorite among many.Bendakaya Pulusu is a flavorful and nutritious dish that has been enjoyed by generations in India. Its simple recipe and easily available ingredients make it a favorite among home cooks. So, next time you want to try something new, give this dish a try and experience the authentic flavors of South Indian cuisine.

Recipe

To make Bendakaya Pulusu, you would require ingredients:

  • 500g fresh okra (bendakaya)
  • 2 medium-sized onions, chopped
  • 2 medium-sized tomatoes, chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit in half
  • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp red chili powder and 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds, 1 tsp fenugreek seeds and 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1/4 cup tamarind pulp
  • 2 tbsp jaggery or sugar
  • 2 tbsp oil and fresh coriander leaves

Procedure:

  1. Wash the okra and pat them dry. Cut them into 1-inch pieces and keep them aside. Heat oil in a deep pan or kadhai and add cumin seeds, mustard seeds, and fenugreek seeds. Let them splutter. Add chopped onions and green chilies to the pan and saut√© until they turn translucent.
  2. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for a minute. Now add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn mushy. Add red chili powder, coriander powder, turmeric powder, and salt to the pan. Mix well and let it cook for 2-3 minutes.
  3. Add the chopped okra to the pan and mix well with the masala. Cook for 5 minutes. In a separate bowl, mix tamarind pulp with 1 cup of water and add it to the pan. Let the curry simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Add jaggery or sugar to the pan and mix well. This will balance out the tanginess from the tamarind and add a subtle sweetness to the dish. Once the okra is cooked and the curry has thickened, turn off the heat. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with rice or roti.

Place of Origin

Bendakaya Pulusu is believed to have originated in the state of Andhra Pradesh, where it is a staple in most households. It is also commonly prepared in the neighboring states of Telangana, Tamil Nadu, and Karnataka. The word 'pulusu' in Telugu translates to 'tamarind-based gravy,' which is the key ingredient in this dish.
